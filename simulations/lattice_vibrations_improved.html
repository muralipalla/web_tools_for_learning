Phonon: collective wave-like lattice motion

In a real solid, atoms do not sit still. They oscillate around equilibrium positions. Temperature measures the average thermal energy of these motions. A phonon is a collective vibration pattern of the lattice.

  • Temperature increases the random vibration strength.
  • Bond stiffness visually tightens or softens the lattice links.
  • Thermal disorder makes neighbouring atoms less synchronized.
  • The phonon switch adds an organized wave on top of random motion.
