Wave Phase, Wavelength and Frequency Explorer

Compare two rotating vectors and their sine/cosine projections. Amplitude is fixed so this applet focuses only on phase, wavelength, and frequency.

Δθ = 1.05 rad Phase difference between the two rotating vectors.
≈ 60.2° Same phase difference in degrees.
λ = 180 px Distance between two successive crests.
f = 1.00 Hz Oscillations per second in this model.

Reading the picture

Cyan vector and cyan wave: reference wave A
Amber vector and amber wave: phase-shifted wave B
Faint guide lines show projection onto axes
A: y = R sin(2πft + φ − 2πx/λ)
B: y = R sin(2πft + φ + Δθ − 2πx/λ)
Fixed amplitude: R = constant

The rotating vectors show the instantaneous phase. The drawn wave to the right uses the wavelength slider, so increasing λ spreads the crests farther apart. Increasing frequency makes the phase advance faster in time.

  • Amplitude is intentionally fixed in this applet.
  • λ controls spatial spacing of the wave pattern.
  • f controls how fast the oscillation evolves.
  • For a traveling wave, wave speed would be v = fλ.
