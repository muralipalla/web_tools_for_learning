Wave Phase Shift Explorer

Compare two rotating vectors and their projections. The horizontal projection produces cosine-like motion and the vertical projection produces sine-like motion. Adjust the phase difference to see how one wave leads or lags the other.

Δθ = 1.05 rad Phase difference between the two rotating vectors.
≈ 60.2° Same phase difference in degrees.

Reading the picture

Cyan vector and cyan wave: reference wave A
Amber vector and amber wave: phase-shifted wave B
Faint guide lines show projection onto axes
A: y = R sin(ωt + φ)
B: y = R sin(ωt + φ + Δθ)
X projection: x = R cos(ωt + φ)

In this representation, uniform circular motion is projected onto straight axes. The projection becomes sinusoidal motion. Changing Δθ shifts the amber wave relative to the cyan wave.

  • Δθ > 0: wave B leads wave A.
  • Δθ < 0: wave B lags wave A.
  • Δθ ≈ π means the two waves are nearly opposite in phase.
