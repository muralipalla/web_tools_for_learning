Parametric Trajectory Visualizer

Enter equations for x(t) and y(t), set a time interval, and watch a point move along the resulting trajectory. Use it for projectile motion, circular motion, Lissajous figures, spirals, and general parametric curves.

t = 0.000 Current value of the parameter.
(x, y) = (0, 0) Instantaneous position on the trajectory.
|v| ≈ 0 Numerical derivative estimate.
Auto-fit: on The graph scales to fit the curve.

Equations

Use either sin(t) or Math.sin(t). Common functions are supported.

How to use

Cyan curve: full trajectory
Red marker: current position
Green arrow: velocity/tangent direction
x = x(t),   y = y(t)
v ≈ (r(t+Δt) − r(t−Δt))/(2Δt)
Projectile preset: y = ut − ½gt²

This is a general parametric-curve visualizer. For projectile motion, enter horizontal and vertical position equations. For circular or oscillatory motion, use sine and cosine.

  • Use t as the parameter.
  • Supported shortcuts include sin, cos, tan, sqrt, abs, exp, log and PI.
  • Click Plot after editing equations or time range.
