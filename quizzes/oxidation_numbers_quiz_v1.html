Quick rules

Sum of oxidation numbers = charge
Neutral compound: sum = 0
Ion: sum = ionic charge

Special cases are important: oxygen is not always −2, hydrogen is −1 in metal hydrides, and average oxidation numbers can be fractional in mixed-valence or polyatomic species.

  • Peroxide: O = −1.
  • Superoxide: O = −1/2.
  • Free element: oxidation number = 0.
  • Complexes: include ligand charges and counterions.
